Abstract: Novel Coronavirus 2019 dataset analysis

The dataset tracks the spread of a recent pandemic and is collected and managed by Johns Hopkins University Center for Systems Science and Engineering (CSSE). It contains different csv files containing various epidemiologic data. For example, there is time series data providing the confirmed, recovered and death counts for different countries given a date. There is also aggregated data combining the time series data for key countries or worldwide. Additionally, there is special us data containing iso standards and case numbers.
The dataset is analysed and insights are documented in this document.

  4. How does the mortality rate differ?
  5. Is there a decrease to the increase observable?

The epidemic usually takes place in three phases: First, there is a phase of exponential growth. Second, there is a phase of linear growth. and third, there is a decline in cases. Usually, it take three weeks from an exponential to a linear growth. The first country to be affected by the coronavirus was china. Following china were Iran and Italy. For Italy, the exponential growth phase lasted about four weeks, for Iran a little bit less. The growth rate in Italy was steeper though. In the end, Italy ends up with a lot more cases than Iran. Following Italy and Iran, France, Germany and Spain were affected. After that the UK and US were affected.
Interesting to nice is, that countries that are affected later, do not tend to be better prepared for the pandemic. It seems like the countries were not able to use the additional time to decrease the spread rate.

The following analysis will focus on the US, as it is the most affected country, and there is special data for it in the provided dataset.

If we observe the death count in a tree map, this fact is even more clear. Almost half of the area is covered by New York only. (New York does not account for half of the deaths in the us, some data appears to be missing.) So why is New York so heavily affected?

On the chart above, the death count per state is depicted as a bar chart. The Size of the bar determines the state population. We can see, that (in a general trend) - the more population a state has, the more people are affected. But the population of California is higher than the one of New York. So maybe, there is only a correlation - not a causal relation - between population and case numbers. The case numbers could be more dependant on the population density. Sadly, population density data is missing in this dataset.

In the boxplot chart, no spreading of the cases can be observed. (States with low case numbers are not getting closer to states that already have high case numbers). Atleast, there is no traffic from urban to rural areas in any significant way. Instead, the case numbers of different states get denser (on a logarythmic scale).

Section 3 - Data quality issues

There are many data quality issues, that make analysing this dataset hard, and the results from the data partly irrelevant. First, not all cases are confirmed, so the difference between confirmed cases and actual cases can vary drasticly. Also, the way of counting confirmed cases also differs from country to country, and is even changed (as for example in china) sometimes. Additionally, there might by false positives or false negatives on corona death cases, and here the way of counting matters as well.
Many additional features would have been interesting to research, but the data for that was missing. For example, correlating population density with amount of death / confirmed cases might be working - but there is no data to confirm it. Further, no good indicators for healthcare power are provided, that might further help to understand why the coronavirus spread the way it did, and certain areas are affected how they are.
All in all though, the situation is generally most likely to complex to analyze with data. There are many geographical features that have an influence on the spread of the virus. Also, there are events that might have spread the virus further. It is not easy to take all that into account. Therefore, the analysis of the coronavirus spread proves to be a hard problem.
